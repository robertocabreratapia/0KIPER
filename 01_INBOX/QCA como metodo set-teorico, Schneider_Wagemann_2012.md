## Idea
La QCA es un método set-teórico orientado a la interpretación causal. Se distingue por el uso de tablas de verdad y por la minimización lógica para identificar condiciones necesarias y suficientes.

## Comentarios
Definición estricta de QCA a partir de sus rasgos distintivos frente a otros métodos set-teóricos.

## Fuente (si la hay)
[@Schneider_Wagemann_2012, 8-9]

“Qualitative Comparative Analysis, commonly known under its acronym QCA, is the methodological tool that is perhaps most directly associated with set theory.”

“QCA distinguishes itself from other set-theoretic approaches by the combined presence of the following features. First, it aims at a causal interpretation. Second, QCA makes use of so-called truth tables. Third, QCA approaches make use of the principles of logical minimization.”
